Capturing Endstates

After generating trials of markov chains, you will have a list of endstates for each trial. Recall that the steady state is also the probability that the chain will end on a certain state after a certain number of trials. Taking the ratio of the endstates for multiple trials will be similar to the probability for the steady state.

Count the appearance of endstates

This function creates two lists: one that records frequencies, and one that sums the frequencies.

  1. Recording the frequency - This allows you to track the amount of time the system spends in a each state. A histogram can be formed to find the average amount of time spent in each state.

  2. Summing the frequencies - This list records the total amount of times the system was in the state. Dividing these times by the number of trials will give you a ratio that reflects the steady state.
