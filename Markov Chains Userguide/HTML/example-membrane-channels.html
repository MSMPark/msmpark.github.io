Membrane Current

Premise

The membrane of a cell opens and closes a channel to allow specific ions to pass through. Suppose that a channel has three different states-- two closed states, one open state. Here are the probabilities associated with each state:

  • C1, the first closed state, has a 98% chance of staying, and a 2% chance of transitioning to C2.

  • C2, the second closed state, has a 10% chance of transitioning to C1, a 70% chance of staying, and a 20% chance of transitioning to O.

  • O, the open state, has a 95% chance of staying, and a 5% chance of transitioning to C2.

Markov Chain

The sequence of transitions will record which opened or closed state the membrane channel is in.

C1, C1, C1, C1, C1, C1, C1, C2, C2, C2, O, O, O, O, O, O. Since C1and O have such a large chance of staying, you will see long strings of each with brief showings of C2.
